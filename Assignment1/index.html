The first thing I paid attention to was the movement of the initial 3D menu when hovering my cursor over each icon.

Q3 What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the the interactive 3D islands and menus to see what would happen with each interaction

Q4 What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action was scrolling to explore either the menus or individual pages

Q5 What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I felt as though the intended goal of the experience was to engage audiences by demonstrating the company's creative talents. It was designed to be both informational about the company while also being an entertaining interactive experience.

Q6 How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

It primarily achieves this effect with the moving, ineractive 3d elements. The dynamic pages that change as you scroll also help to achieve this, keeping the user engaged with changing elements that convey the company's messages while maintaining engagement.

Q7 What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I get the impression that the experience should be interacted with for around 20 minutes as the user explores the full webste. It may be experienced multiple times if the user needs something different out of each visit.

Q8 How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

It communicated that it should be interacted with for a significant amount of time, as each individual page tended to lead into another once the user reached the end, guiding them to stay in the experience for longer and explore everything.

Q9 What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience references other media forms such as 3D design, photography, digital art.

Q10 What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The moving, responsive 3D media references communicate that you should take the time to interact with it to experience it to the fullest.

Q11 What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

It evokes a feeling of curiousity, leading you to wonder how the various elements will respond to interaction.

Q12 What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction was some of the navigation. Because of all the various styles of interaction and layout throughout the pages, backtracking and finding specific information could at times be difficult.

Q13 What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction was how certain images and elements would move in response to the cursor. This is because it made the interaction feel responsive and dynamic.
